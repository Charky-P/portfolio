UNSW Course Review Summaries

Course Rating Breakdown

I rate each course based off 4 factors, difficulty, usefulness, workload and overall rating. The difficulty rating is how hard the course was to understand and based off the assignment difficulties, note that this rating considers the time it is usually taken, for example a first year course might be rated similar to a third year course, even though it is easier. Usefulness is determined by how much is the content used outside the course, after completion, this can be through real application in a job, or it being a major requirement or follow through to other courses, this rating is basically how much I recommend this course if you care about learning. The workload rating is straightfoward, how many hours is required, this can be based off contact hours and time taken for assignments or studying for tests and the exam. The overall rating is just all three factors combined, along with other details such as teaching quality, assignment quality and enjoyability.

Course Ratings & Summaries

COMP1511 -

Overall: 10/10 | Difficulty: 6/10 | Usefulness: 9/10 | Workload: 5/10

COMP1511 (Programming Fundamentals) is the first computer science course taken, which teaches C and basic data structures like arrays and linked lists. The course is one of the best and gives you a great introduction to programming, as C is a low-level language, allowing for more control. Here is my full review.

MATH1131 -

Overall: 8/10 | Difficulty: 7/10 | Usefulness: 7/10 | Workload: 7/10

MATH1131 (Mathematics 1A) is the first mathematics course taken, alternatively MATH1141 can be taken, which is the higher version. The course teaches two components, linear algebra and calculus along with Maple, a mathematical computing software. The course is basically a recap of highschool content, along with a few new things taught more rigorously and in depth, knowledge of mathematics extension 1 and 2 is extremely helpful, but not required. Here is my full review.

COMP1521 -

Overall: 6/10 | Difficulty: 7/10 | Usefulness: 5/10 | Workload: 5/10

COMP1521 (Computer Systems Fundamentals) is a core course to a computer science degree, is one of three level one core COMP courses, usually taken after COMP1511. The course is relatively challenging, as it teaches an assembly language, MIPS and lower level abstractions such as bitwise operators, processes and threads. The course is low on workload, however most computer science students dread this course as deviates from easier coding conventions to show what occurs under the hood for a programming language. MATH1231 -

Overall: 8/10 | Difficulty: 6/10 | Usefulness: 7/10 | Workload: 6/10

MATH1231 (Mathematics 1B) is the continuation of MATH1131 (Mathematics 1A) and is quite similar to MATH1131, however most students find MATH1231 to generally be easier since the mistakes made in MATH1131 won't carry over, since the two courses are so similar. The course covers algebra, calculus and Maple mathematical computing, and in addition it contains an introduction to statistics. Here is the full review.

COMP2521 (Data Structures And Algorithms) is essential to a computer scientist, and is an extremely tedious course if taken first year. Taking MATH1081 (Discrete Mathematics) can help a lot, however it goes more in depth than required. COMP2521 is not taught the best, which makes it harder than it should be, but the content learnt is intriguing and useful to other courses. Here is the full review.

MATH1081 -

Overall: 8/10 | Difficulty: 8/10 | Usefulness: 8/10 | Workload: 6/10

MATH1081 (Discrete Mathematics) is a core course and is fundamental to computer scientists, it essentially covers the theory of COMP2521 in more depth, however it not necessary before taking COMP2521. The course covers set theory, number theory, proofs and logic, combinatorics and graph theory, and is a standard first year mathematics course, with high teaching quality and is much more interesting than MATH1131, yet also harder.
