COMP2521 Course Review

Overview

COMP2521 or Data Structures and Algorithms is a second level course which is usually taken first or second year, after taking COMP1511. It is a core course to computer science students and covers essential content relating to computer scientists, by learning how to create efficient algorithms and code. Taking this course with or after MATH1081 will greatly help, as MATH1081 and COMP2521 overlap in their graph section, however note that MATH1081 is much more in depth with graph theory. Check out the COMP2521 handbook and course outline for more information and see this blog post on what my ratings are based on.

Ratings

  • Overall: 7/10
  • Difficulty: 8/10
  • Usefulness: 9/10
  • Workload: 8/10

Course Details

  • Course Name: Data Structures and Algorithms
  • Term Taken: 24T2
  • Prerequisites: COMP1511
  • Final Mark: 93
  • Contact Hours: Two 2-hour lectures, One TLB (1-hour tutorial, 2-hour lab) per week
  • Assessment Structure:
    • Labs (15%)
    • Quizzes (10%)
    • Assignment 1 (15%)
    • Assignment 2 (15%)
    • Exam (45%)
  • Content: Time Complexity, Sorting Algorithms, Data Structures, Trees, Graphs

Critique

Tutorials and Lectures

The tutorials and lectures are structured exactly like COMP1511, however I found the lecturer I had (Sim Mautner) was extremely disorganised and boring to watch, I would recommend watching Hayden Smith's lectures, or doing what I did, which was to just use the lecture slides which is all you need, as I stopped watching their lectures after the first one. The tutorials are decent, similar to COMP1511 and aren't mandatory, I had a pretty good tutor, however do note that you will have to interact with your tutors more than in COMP1511 due to the labs being manually marked, more information in the next part.

Labs

The labs can be quite time consuming and there are no auto tests like in COMP1511, so making silly mistakes is very easy, and I was unable to get the full lab marks, which took your best 7 labs out of the 8. Do note that the labs and everything in this course uses C, or pseudocode. The labs were quite tedious, however did teach you a lot, but the main downside was them being manually marked, which meant if you had a lenient tutor you could get full marks easily, conversely a bad tutor is a hassle to deal with. If you do happen to have a bad tutor, you are still able to get marked by another tutor in a different lab, however I would advise not doing this unless you have to. Each lab which is given each week allows for an extra two weeks for it to be manually marked, so you do not have to attend every lab, I think this part of the course was to deal with the usage of AI, since you are expected to explain your solution, however it also punishes silly mistakes, as most of the labs consist of a manual and auto mark. The last few labs are quite challenging too, as you have to use graphs, implementing DFS and BFS.

Quizzes

The quizzes are weekly starting from the second week, and only the best 6 of 8 count, you can find these quizzes on the website on WebCMS3. The quizzes are extremely frustrating, as there a lot of trick questions making it hard to get full marks, luckily you can change your answers whenever, and have room for 2 failed quizzes, do not underestimate the quizzes, but they are only worth 10% so they are less of a big deal than the labs. A trick is to find some of the quizzes on the internet, and they usually reuse the questions.

Assignments

I found the assignments quite straightforward and easy, as long as you know how to use the data structures like trees in assignment 1 and graphs in assignment 2. Assignment 1 for me was a Huffman tree encoding, do note that style does apply still such as comments including comments in the ADT's file. Assignment 2 had 4 subsets, with a challenge in subset 1 which was creating a Big Oh of n solution (limited time complexity for your solution). Subset 4 was quite challenging and required abstract thinking, however these assignments are only weighed 15%.

Exam

The exam was easier than expected, however there are mixed opinions as many people failed. The exam is composed of two sections, a theory section which is the first 4 to 8 questions, followed by the practical section. The theory section I found harder, and usually just required worded explanations on basic questions, the practical question on the other hand was just programming questions. The best way to describe the exam was that the theory was like a quiz, and the programming was like a lab. I would study the programming part more, since it is weighed more, however there are hurdles for both sections. There is also a example exam given, which is easier than the actual exam, so don't expect the difficulty to match. This course failed a lot of people, and is a big step up, mostly due to the exam hurdles, so if you are not confident, and did not perform that well in COMP1511 (Distinction or higher), then you will struggle in this course, and it is better to take in second year. You should be confident in DFS and BFS, and most of the theory before the exam.
